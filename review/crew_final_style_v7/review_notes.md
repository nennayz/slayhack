## Style Target

Preferred direction for the next pass:

- realistic cinematic 3D
- glossy semi-realistic AAA fashion-game render
- visible skin texture, pores, realistic hair, leather grain, and metal wear
- sexy fashion editorial styling without explicit posing or nudity
- non-cartoon faces and proportions
- distinct faces, hair silhouettes, props, and role signals

## Ensemble Verdict

The v7 set is the strongest current roster-wide direction. It has the right
premium pirate-fleet world, detailed fashion materials, strong lighting, and a
clearer full-team silhouette than the earlier portrait direction.

Recommended path: keep v7 as the current review anchor, but do not promote it
to `static/crew/` yet.

Reason: several characters are close enough to lock, while a few need targeted
identity corrections before the full set becomes canonical.

Risk if skipped: live assets could lock in role confusion, especially around
Captain authority, PM identity, and the intelligence/community/concept roles.

Smallest safe step: approve or reject the character-specific notes below, then
revise only the marked characters.

## Cohesion Notes

What to preserve:

- full-body runway composition
- ship deck / command room / specialist workstation settings
- glossy black leather, gold trim, metal scratches, wet deck reflections
- role props in-hand, not hidden in the background
- distinct palettes: black-gold Captain, black-crimson Slay, emerald Robin,
  sky/blue Mia, coral Zoe, wine Bella, violet Lila, teal Vera, silver Nora,
  lime Roxy, rose Emma, cobalt-lime Iris, lavender-brass Sage, seafoam Nami,
  violet-starlight Genie

What to avoid:

- anime/cute cartoon proportions
- same-face glam across the women
- generic fantasy mage props with no workflow role
- black-gold command compass on non-Captain characters
- promoting concept-only characters as live workflow owners before boundaries exist
